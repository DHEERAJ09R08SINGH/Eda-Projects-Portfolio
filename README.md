# 📊 Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) Projects Portfolio

## 📌 Overview

This repository contains a collection of **Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) projects** performed on multiple real-world datasets using Python.

The purpose of these projects is to understand datasets, identify patterns, detect anomalies, and extract meaningful insights through **data visualization and statistical analysis**.

EDA is a critical step in the **data science workflow**, helping build better machine learning models and data-driven decisions.

# 📁 Projects Included

## 1️⃣ Red Wine Quality Analysis

**Objective**

Analyze the relationship between chemical properties of red wine and the resulting wine quality.

**Key Analysis**

* Feature distribution analysis
* Correlation heatmap
* Alcohol vs quality relationship
* Outlier detection

**Key Insight**

Higher alcohol content tends to correlate with better wine quality.

---

## 2️⃣ Student Performance Analysis

**Objective**

Explore factors affecting students' academic performance.

**Key Analysis**

* Score distribution analysis
* Gender vs score comparison
* Parental education impact
* Correlation between subjects

**Key Insight**

Math, reading, and writing scores show strong correlation.

---

## 3️⃣ Forest Fire Dataset Analysis

**Objective**

Analyze environmental conditions that contribute to forest fire spread.

**Key Analysis**

* Temperature vs burned area
* Wind vs fire spread
* Distribution of burned area
* Correlation between environmental variables

**Key Insight**

High temperature and wind speed increase fire risk.

---

## 4️⃣ Flight Price Dataset Analysis

**Objective**

Identify factors that influence airline ticket prices.

**Key Analysis**

* Airline vs price comparison
* Stops vs ticket price
* Month vs price trend
* Flight duration vs price

**Key Insight**

Flights with more stops generally have lower ticket prices.

---

## 5️⃣ Google Playstore Dataset Analysis

**Objective**

Analyze trends in mobile applications available on the Google Playstore.

**Key Analysis**

* App category distribution
* Rating vs installs relationship
* Free vs paid apps comparison
* Popular categories

**Key Insight**

Free apps typically receive significantly more installs than paid apps.
